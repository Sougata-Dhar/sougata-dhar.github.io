About Me

Hello and welcome to my homepage. I am an Assistant Professor at the School of Computing and Data Science in the Wentworth Institute of Technology. Prior to this, I spend three years as a visiting assistant professor in the Department of Mathematics at UConn and two years at the University of Maine. I completed my Ph.D in Mathematics under the supervision of Prof. Qingkai Kong from Northern Illinois University in 2017.

I work primarily in the intersection of analysis and differential equations. I enjoy having conversations about mathematical and functional inequalities and their applications to differential equations. Lately, I started to look into nonlinear partial differential equations and eigenvalue problems. I am always happy to chat about my work and interested to know about my peers work. If you are interested, please feel free to to contact me. If you are a student and interested to work with me, please send me an email with your interest and background and we can have a conversation about that.

Beside mathematics, I enjoy hiking, rock climbing, and traveling. I have traveled to 40 states within the USA and I envision being a member of the 50 state club someday. I am a gastronomer and enjoy seafood the most (living in New England helps!). Italian and Japanese are two of my favorite cuisines- for me, distance is a mere number for fine cheese and delectable sushi! Other than the food, I am passionate about dark roast coffee, perfume, and single malt scotch. Cheers to that!
