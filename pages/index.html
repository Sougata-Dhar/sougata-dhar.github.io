About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of Applied Mathematics at the School of Computing and Data Science in the Wentworth Institute of Technology.

I completed my Ph.D. at the Northern Illinois University under the supervision of Professor Qingkai Kong in 2017. I received my Masters in Mathematics from University of Texas Pan American in 2011 and my Bachelors from the Techno India of Saltlake, India in 2008. Prior to moving to Wentworth, I had a 2-year postdoctoral position at the University of Maine and a 3-year postdoctoral position at the University of Connecticut.

I work primarily in the intersection of analysis and differential equations. I enjoy having conversations about mathematical and functional inequalities and their applications to differential equations. Lately, I started to look into nonlinear partial differential equations and eigenvalue problems. I am always happy to chat about my work and interested to know about my peers work. If you are interested, please feel free to to contact me. If you are a student and interested to work with me, please send me an email with your interest and background and we can have a conversation about that.

Beside mathematics, I enjoy physical activities and traveling. I have traveled to 42 states within the USA and I envision being a member of the 50 state club someday. I am a strong believer of prioritizing mental health. Finally, I am a gastronomer and enjoy seafood the most. On that note, Italian and Japanese are two of my favorite cuisines- for me, distance is a mere number for fine cheese and delectable sushi! Other than the food, I am passionate about dark roast coffee, perfume, and scotch. Cheers to that!
